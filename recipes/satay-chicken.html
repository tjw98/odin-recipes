Recipe for Satay Chicken

Authentic Thai Chicken Satay with Peanut Sauce

Savor the exquisite allure of our Satay Chicken, a culinary masterpiece that encapsulates the essence of Southeast Asian cuisine. Succulent chicken skewers, bathed in a symphony of spices and coconut milk, deliver a perfect balance of savory and sweet. The highlight is the luscious peanut sauce, a velvety blend that adds a layer of richness and depth. With each bite, experience the tantalizing melding of flavors, accentuated by a subtle smokiness. Whether enjoyed as a delightful appetizer or a flavorful main course, our Satay Chicken promises a taste adventure that transports you to the vibrant and aromatic streets of the region. Elevate your dining experience with this irresistible dish that captures the true spirit of satay.

Ingredients

  • Coconut milk
  • Red curry paste
  • Cider or white vinegar (not lime juice!)
  • Pure peanut butter
  • Soy sauce
  • Sugar and salt

Method

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with 2 tablespoons of red curry paste. Ensure the chicken is well-coated and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the coconut milk, remaining red curry paste, cider or white vinegar, peanut butter, soy sauce, sugar, and salt. Stir continuously until the mixture is well-blended and smooth. Simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly. Set aside.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, remember to soak them in water for about 30 minutes to prevent burning during cooking.
  4. Grill the skewers on a preheated grill or use an oven broiler for approximately 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char.
  5. Arrange the skewers on a platter and generously drizzle the prepared satay sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped peanuts and fresh cilantro if desired.
  6. Dive into the exquisite flavors of your homemade Satay Chicken. Serve with steamed rice or as part of a larger Asian-inspired feast. The creamy, savory, and slightly tangy notes of the satay sauce will make this dish a favorite at your dining table.
